Transforming Roads into Smart Energy Generators

We present a sustainable and innovative solution to the global energy crisis by converting mechanical energy from road traffic into electricity using Triboelectric Nanogenerators (TENGs). This advanced yet cost-effective technology captures everyday motion—such as vehicles and pedestrians on asphalt—and transforms it into clean, storable energy. Every step, every wheel rotation becomes a source of renewable power.

Why TENG?

Energy from Everyday Motion

TENGs (Triboelectric Nanogenerators) capture mechanical energy from routine activities—like walking or driving—converting it into clean, usable electricity. This makes every movement a potential source of sustainable power.

Low-Cost & Scalable

TENG technology uses inexpensive, flexible materials and doesn’t rely on rare or heavy metals. It can be scaled from small sensors to large energy-harvesting surfaces embedded in roads and buildings.

Eco-Friendly & Versatile

With no harmful emissions or complex installation, TENGs offer a green solution adaptable to urban infrastructure, wearable tech, and even remote environments where traditional power sources are impractical.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

A TENG is an energy harvesting device that converts mechanical energy from motion or vibration into electricity using the triboelectric effect (contact electrification) and electrostatic induction.

TENGs generate electricity when two dissimilar materials come into contact and then separate, creating a charge imbalance. This mechanical energy from motion is converted to electrical energy through electrostatic induction.

TENGs can be used in:

  • Self-powered sensors and IoT devices
  • Wearable electronics
  • Road energy harvesting (smart roads)
  • Ocean wave energy harvesting
  • Biomedical devices
  • Industrial condition monitoring

  • Works with low-frequency mechanical energy
  • Lightweight and flexible designs possible
  • Can be made from low-cost materials
  • Environmentally friendly energy harvesting
  • Doesn't require external power sources

Modern TENGs can achieve energy conversion efficiencies of 50-85%, depending on materials and design. Efficiency continues to improve with ongoing research.

Common materials include:

  • Polymers (PTFE, PDMS, FEP)
  • Metals (copper, aluminum)
  • Nanocomposites
  • Eco-friendly/biodegradable materials
